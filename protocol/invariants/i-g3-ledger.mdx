# I-G3 — Ledger primacy

## Statement

For every action A executed by the Protocol, the corresponding Decision D is committed to the Evidence Ledger before the first side-effecting operation of A's execution begins.

The ledger is the source of truth. If it isn't in the ledger, it didn't happen.

## Why

Without ledger primacy, audit becomes best-effort. With ledger primacy, every action that occurred is reconstructable from the ledger alone, and every decision is provably attributable to the agent, the policy, and the approver (if any).

This is the property that makes regulated deployments (HIPAA workflows, attorney work-product, financial settlement) tractable. The Protocol does not say "we believe we logged everything." It says "the action could not have proceeded if it wasn't in the ledger first."

## What enforces it

- **Engine's commit-then-execute order.** The execution path in `@sint/engine/runtime.ts` calls `ledger.append(decision)` synchronously before dispatching to the Bridge adapter. The dispatch awaits the append's `Result.Ok`.
- **Database-level INSERT-only.** The `ledger_entries` table has a trigger that rejects UPDATE and DELETE. Soft-deletes attempted via direct DB access are themselves ledgered as `tamper_attempt` entries.
- **Hash chain verification.** A continuous job recomputes the chain every 10 minutes and compares against an independent Redis index. Any divergence pages on-call.
- **Property test:** `pnpm run test:invariants:g3` — generates 5,000 randomized action sequences; for each, asserts the ledger entry for the decision precedes the side effect's timestamp.

## What a violation looks like

A violation would mean an action's side effect occurred without a preceding ledger entry. Three failure modes the property tests cover:

1. **Out-of-order writes.** If Engine's runtime were rewritten to dispatch asynchronously, the side effect could race the append. Property tests catch this.
2. **Bypassed ledger.** A code path that calls the Bridge adapter directly without going through `runtime.execute()`. CodeQL rules in CI flag any such path.
3. **Soft-deleted entries.** The DB-level INSERT-only trigger blocks this; a manual `DELETE` attempt produces a `tamper_attempt` ledger entry and an alert.

If a violation is observed in production, treat as P0: pause writes for the tenant, run full chain verification, audit the ledger for missing entries, escalate.

## Three things this guarantees

1. **Reconstructibility.** Given only the ledger, you can reconstruct every action that occurred.
2. **Attribution.** Every side effect maps to a decision, a policy, an agent, and (for T2+) an approver.
3. **Tamper evidence.** Any modification of historical entries breaks the hash chain and is detected within 10 minutes.

## What this does not guarantee

- **No information was leaked.** I-G3 covers actions, not all information flow. Read paths (data viewed by the agent en route to a decision) are out of scope unless the policy declares them ledgered.
- **The decision was correct.** I-G3 says the decision was recorded. Whether the decision was sound is a function of the policy, not the ledger.
- **The actuator complied.** I-G3 says Engine dispatched the action. Whether the actuator faithfully executed is a property of the actuator and the Bridge adapter's execution check.
