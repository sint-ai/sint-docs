# CMO Operator

**One-liner:** Reads your positioning brief. Produces content. Distributes it across owned channels. Measures what moved. Iterates on a schedule.

## What it replaces

- Fractional CMO or agency retainer for content production + distribution
- Social scheduling tools + the humans who feed them
- The recurring "we should be posting more" conversation

## System

```
Positioning brief (in Canon)
   ▼
Content planner
   ▼
Draft (long-form, social, email)
   ▼
Review (T2) — founder voice, jurisdictional claims
   ▼
Publish (LinkedIn, X, blog, newsletter)
   ▼
Metrics ingest
   ▼
Weekly report
   ▼
Brief-update loop
```

## Safety envelope

| Tier | Action classes |
|---|---|
| T0 | `canon.brief.read`, `metrics.read`, `competitor.read` |
| T1 | `plan.week`, `draft.longform`, `draft.social`, `publish.x.thread`, `publish.blog`, `publish.newsletter` |
| T2 | `draft.founder-quote`, `publish.linkedin` (founder account), any ad-hoc publish |
| T3 | Any response to a third-party mention |
| T4 | Any claim touching regulated language (medical, financial, legal) |

Hard limits:

- Never publish under a founder account without founder T2 approval
- Never make pricing claims
- Never respond to a DM autonomously
- Zero dark patterns (no fake scarcity, no fabricated testimonials)

## Metrics

- Publishing cadence hit-rate
- Engagement per channel
- Qualified traffic to /contact
- Brand-consistency score (internal rubric)
- Time saved vs prior-state agency or in-house

## Pricing band

$7k–$18k/mo retainer + 3–8% outcome share on qualified inbound.
